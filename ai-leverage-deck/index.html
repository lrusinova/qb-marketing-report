A 70-person marketing org operating across three business units, seven strategic priorities, and multiple active programs each quarter — with two PMs and no dedicated operations headcount.

The traditional answer is to hire more. Instead, I used AI to build the operating infrastructure that makes the whole program run — and then used that same infrastructure to report on it, automate it, and scale it.

Industry standard for this scale: 4–5 PMs + a dedicated marketing ops resource.
We have 2. AI closes that gap.

AI Agents & Knowledge Layer

Jira + Confluence structured as a machine-readable knowledge base
  • Rovo Marketing PM Agent — trained on the full Confluence knowledge base; answers program questions, status queries, and routing questions in natural language
  • Structured data layer — every issue, label, component, and initiative follows consistent naming so AI agents can query across the entire program without ambiguity
  • Claude as co-PM — used for Jira issue creation, Confluence writing, bulk updates, data analysis, dashboard builds, and automation design
  • Extending to Content & Creative PM — the same AI-augmented workflow is now being rolled out to Soumya (Content & Creative PM) — one PM building the playbook for two

The Bigger Picture: Jira + Confluence as an LLM

This is not just project management — it is a structured knowledge base that AI agents can learn from and act on
    • Every initiative, epic, and task is named consistently — AI can parse it without ambiguity
    • Every deliverable is labeled by BU, channel, and quarter — AI can slice any dimension instantly
    • Confluence is the documented "why" behind every structure decision — AI agents have context, not just data
    • Together, this replaces weeks of manual status gathering with a real-time, queryable program brain
